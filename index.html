Abstract

3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) has emerged as a promising technique for novel view synthesis in computer graphics, offering superior flexibility and quality compared to traditional voxel-based and mesh-based methods. However, strong artifacts can be observed when changing the sampling rate, eg., by changing focal length or camera distance; In addition, all Gaussians within the field of view need to participate in rendering, leading to a lack of flexibility in the rendering approach. To address these challenges, we propose a novel hierarchical 3DGS representation that establishes a LOD (Level of Detail) structure for the scene. During the model training phase, we create different layers for various sampling rates, with higher GS layers derived from lower ones. In the rendering phase, our method adaptively selects the Gaussians involved in rendering based on the sampling rate, thereby enhancing rendering efficiency. Additionally, we establish recursive relationships between different levels, allowing higher layers to be inferred from lower layers, significantly reducing model storage requirements. In summary, our approach demonstrates significant improvements in both rendering quality and model storage efficiency compared to existing methods.
